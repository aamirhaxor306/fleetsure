Industry Updates

What's Happening in Indian Fleet Compliance

Government crackdowns and new regulations are reshaping fleet operations. Stay ahead or pay the price.

Enforcement

ANPR Cameras Going Live Across Highways

AI-powered cameras now auto-detect expired insurance, PUC & permits in real-time. No stopping needed — fines issued instantly via SMS. Already active in Delhi, Mumbai, and 12+ state highways.

Impact: Instant Rs 2,000–10,000 e-challans
Policy

Vehicle Scrappage Policy Now Active

Commercial vehicles older than 15 years must pass mandatory fitness tests at automated centres. Failing vehicles face deregistration. Fitness certificate tracking is now critical.

Impact: ~10 lakh trucks at risk of deregistration
Emission

BS-VI Stage II Norms Now Mandatory

All new commercial vehicles must comply with BS-VI Phase 2 emission standards. Stricter OBD-II monitoring means PUC failures are more likely for older fleets — renewals need closer tracking.

Impact: Higher PUC rejection rate for older trucks
Opportunity

Rs 11.11 Lakh Cr Budget for Infrastructure

Government's massive infra spending means more freight demand and more compliance pressure. Fleet operators expanding now need systems to manage compliance at scale — or risk falling behind.

Opportunity: Fleet demand growing 8-10% annually

Sources: Ministry of Road Transport & Highways, Union Budget 2025-26, SIAM India
